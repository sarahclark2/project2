2 0 1 8 P R I D E P A R A D E

The history of pride in this country runs deep. Many of those in the LGBTQ community continue to face prejudice and violence today. In North Carolina, the first pride parade took place in 1981. The parade was created in response to violent prejudice against the anyone who is not heterosexual or presents themselves that way. Five years prior to the first parade, a man was mistaken to be a homosexual, and was beaten to death. This sparked the pride movement and the fight for justice. This parade continues today, and is still widely celebrated throughout the state, not just in Durham. However, the parade did not occur last year, due to scheduling errors resulting in the parade occuring during Yom Kippur. There was backlash in response to the poor scheduling, and the whole parade fell through. Fast forward almost forty years later, and the legacy of pride continues to be followed and celebrated. Massive crowds gather in Durham around 9th street to celebrate and support their community. This story follows a group of middle school students from Carolina Friends School in a gorup called GLOW. GLOW stands for Gay Lesbian or Whatever, and was formed about 7 years ago as the state's first official middle school queer-straight-alliance. These middle school kids, joined by friends from other schools around the area, are from Carolina Friends School.
